Cat's Eye

Game link

Latest work date: 16/10/2022 | Aprox. dev time: 40 hours

I made this game together with six other devs during the Global Game Jam 2022. The theme for the game jam was duality and that is why we created a game in which the player can look into an alternate world.

The game revolves around collecting cat statues in order to revive a cat you've lost in the past. These statues can only be seen while looking into the alternate world, unfortunately for you it seems like you are not alone there...

Responsibilities:

- Lead Level Designer
- Game Designer
- UI & HUD
- Scene transitions

Design thoughts:

Initial idea was to create a thick spooky forest with some recognizable landmarks put around the map.
This was not fully realised due to performance issues and the density of the forest had to be cut down significantly.
To compensate for the empty spaces a lot of bulky assets were placed around to fill up the space.

I'm the most satisfied with the cat paw landmark. It's a rock formation that resembles a cats paw, I stumbled upon this idea while playing around with our boulder prefabs and thought that it fits the premise and the atmosphere perfectly.

October 2022 Update: I've gone back to the project to investigate the performance issues after getting a tip about what the cause may be.
After fiddling with shadow settings, changing to the Deferred Shading and baking the lightmaps the performance increased by almost 50%!
Note to self: Always try the game with shadows disabled when investigating performance issues c:

Unreal Engine 5 Project - The Foundry

This was a hobby project I have worked on for a month whenever I had time. The goal was to practice Greyboxing and Unreal Engine 5.
I've created an FPS level meant for a Capture-The-Flag gamemode, the level is inspired by games like Valorant and CSGO. In my design I assume that the players are able to use grenades or abilities to smoke off areas.
I'm a huge fan of sniping and lurking in FPS games and so I've designed a map that tries to focus on those elements, to keep things interesting I've added some verticality to the level. The general design of the map is that it is split into three main areas: Mid, Parking and Office.

The Flag Zones are located directly opposite of each other and are very similar in strucutre, only difference being some of the obstacles are in different spots.
I wanted to create an asymmetrical level but I still wanted the core area to have some resemblence to each other.

Mid is designed in a way that allows the snipers to really lockdown the areas that allow fast access to the flag zones. It is deliberately open and is meant to be a high risk high reward section of the map.
Directly opposite of each other are two what I like to call Boiler rooms that provide the snipers with some cover and a good outlook on the Mid section.

Since the Mid section is sandwiched between the Parking and the Office I've added connectors that allow the players to go between the sections.
One of the connectors is located on the Balcony which can be reached either through the Office or by using stairs on the opposite ends of each other.
Below the Balcony there is an alcove Under that provides some cover to players that want to push Mid.

The Top section of Mid can be reached by stairs from both sides of the map.
To cross over to the other side of Top the players would need to go across the Bridge which is the most open section of the map.

Both the Parking and the Office section have a little area that connects them to spawns, these serve as connecting blocks between the main section of the map.

The Office is meant to be the main close-quarters section of the map, there are a lot of corners to watch out for as well as a few spots players can jump up on to get a better overview.
The lighting in the Office is meant to be a cold opposite to the warmth of the Tunnel. I always imagine offices being lit up by a white-blueish light. This gave me an opportunity to fiddle with Post-Processing.

The Parking section of the map is inspired by the classic map Nuke Town. Two buildings opposite of each other with a large obstacle(car) in the middle for some cover and a smaller obstacle on the side.
This section of the map is deliberately done in a simple way due to the complexity of the Office section. I did not want the map to be overly complex.

The Tunnel offers a fast way across Mid, hidden from the snipers but it is a very compact section meaning that if a player would be caught running across they would be very exposed.
